Books I’ve read (and loved) that shape my thoughts on design

Reading has been incredibly influential for my growth as a designer, teaching me critical skills and tips, challenging me with new perspectives, and fueling a passion for design.

What I love most about books is that it allows me to learn from the greats—the likes of Michael Beirut and Josef Muller-Brockman—as if they were my personal tutor communicating lifetimes of experience and wisdom directly to me.

The titles below have been particularly influential and enlightening to me:

Design History

  • Meggs’ History of Graphic Design
    Phillip B. Meggs

  • History of Modern Design
    David Raziman

Visual Identity and Branding

  • Logo Design Love: A Guide to Creating Iconic Brand Identities
    David Airey

  • Designing Brand Identity: An Essential Guide for the Whole Branding Team
    Alina Wheeler

Design Practice

  • How to Be a Graphic Designer without Losing Your Soul
    Adrian Shaughnessy

  • How to Think Like a Great Graphic Designer
    Debbie Millman

  • Burn Your Portfolio: Stuff they don’t teach you in design school, but should
    Michael Janda

  • Steal Like an Artist: 10 Things Nobody Told You About Being Creative
    Austin Kleon

Graphic Design Theory

  • Picture This How Pictures Work
    Molly Bang

  • The Language of Graphic Design: An Illustrated Handbook for Understanding Fundamental Design Principles
    Richard Poulin

  • Graphic Design, Referenced: A Visual Guide to the Language, Applications, and History of Graphic Design
    Bryony Gomez-Palacio, Armin Vit

Interactive Design

  • Above the Fold: Understanding the Principles of Successful Web Site Design
    Brian Miller

  • Smashing Book 5: Real-Life Responsive Web Design
    Multiple Authors

  • The Smashing Book #2
    Multiple Authors

  • A Practical Guide to Designing for the Web
    Mark Boulton

  • The Principles of Beautiful Web Design: Designing Great Web Sites is Not Rocket Science!
    Jason Beaird, James George

Usability

  • The Design of Everyday Things
    Don Norman

  • Don’t Make Me Think: A Common Sense Approach to Web Usability
    Steve Krug

Psychology

  • 100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People
    Susan Weinschenk

User Experience

  • Designing for Emotion

User Research

  • Contextual Design: Design for Life

Typography

    Stephen Coles

    Ellen Lupton

  • On Web Typography
    Jason Santa Maria

  • Inside Paragraphs Typographic Fundamentals
    Cyrus Highsmith

Grids

  • Layout Essentials: 100 Design Principles for Using Grids

  • The Grid: A Modular System for the Design and Production of Newpapers, Magazines, and Books
